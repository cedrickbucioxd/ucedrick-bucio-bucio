During my Senior High School years, I gained hands-on experience in building and rebuilding desktop computers, which sparked my passion for Information Technology. One of the most memorable projects was assembling a PC from scratch, where I learned to identify and properly install components like the motherboard, CPU, RAM, and storage. At first, the process was challenging, especially with cable management and avoiding hardware damage. But with practice, I became more confident in assembling parts, applying thermal paste, and configuring BIOS settings. I also had the chance to troubleshoot older systems—cleaning, upgrading components, and reinstalling operating systems—deepening my understanding of hardware compatibility and system optimization.
